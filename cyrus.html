RPW 1.1 – Section B

Details from the text & plausible inferences

DetailsInferences
A “massive” coral bleaching is happening in Calatagan, Batangas and other parts of the western Philippines.The problem is widespread and not limited to one small area, so marine ecosystems are seriously threatened.
Coral bleaching is caused by an increase in sea surface temperature.Global warming is directly harming marine life, especially corals that are sensitive to heat.
Corals turned white at depths of 3 to 10 feet and were in worse condition than in May.The bleaching is ongoing and worsening, not a short-term event.
The bleaching affected the entire 72-kilometer shoreline of the Calatagan Peninsula.Local fishing and tourism may be negatively affected because coral reefs support livelihoods.
Similar massive bleaching occurred in 2014, 2012, and 2007.Coral bleaching is a recurring problem, suggesting long-term environmental stress.
Marine conservationists blame global warming and pollution such as coal-fired plants and open-dump burning.Human activities are a major contributor, and reducing pollution could help lessen future bleaching.
